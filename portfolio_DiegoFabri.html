06 — Master Thesis
DigiKnitting
Additive Textile Manufacturing — Computational Design — Open Source
DigiKnitting: A Three-Dimensional Approach

A master thesis exploring an emerging additive manufacturing technique that combines the layer-by-layer logic of 3D printing with the loop structure of knitting to build solid, three-dimensional objects from yarn. Currently at TRL 3, no accessible design software, no reproducible fabrication method and limited material understanding exist for volumetric knitting.

Three Interconnected Domains
01 — Material Research

Material experiments establish how stitch topology affects structural compliance and stiffness. These design rules are embedded as a live stiffness simulation in the digital tool, enabling data-driven material selection and volumetric structure optimization.

02 — Voxel Knitter (Digital Tool)

Treats each knit stitch as a voxel in a 3D grid with five assignable types per cell: cast-on, knit, purl, tuck, and connector. This approach significantly lowers the cognitive barrier, allowing designers to develop volumetric knitted structures without deep specialist knitting knowledge.

03 — Mechanical Fabrication System

A modular mechanical pin system with a configurable needle bed makes solid knitting reproducible by hand production. This opens reproducible pathways toward both manual craftsmanship and future machine automation, democratizing access to volumetric knitting.

Research Contribution

Together the three domains form an integrated open-source framework contributing tools, methodologies and design guidelines toward the democratisation of volumetric knitting. Supervised by Jouke Verlinden & Stijn Verwulgen — UAntwerp 2025–2026.

Accepted for publication and presentation at AHFE 2026, Istanbul.
